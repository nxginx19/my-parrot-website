What is a Parrot

Parrots: Parrots (Psittaciformes), also known as psittacines (/ˈsɪtəsaɪnz/), are birds with a strong curved beak, upright stance, and clawed feet. They are classified in four families that contain roughly 410 species in 101 genera, found mostly in tropical and subtropical regions.

THE 4 FAMILIES OF PARROTS

Psittacidae: True parrots

Cacatuidae: Cockatoos

Strigopidae: New Zealand parrots (including the Kakapo)

Nestoridae: New Zealand parrots (including Kea and Kaka)

Information about Parrots

  1. Intelligence:
      2. Parrots are among the most intelligent birds
      They can mimic human speech and solve complex problems

  2. Diet:
      3. Parrots mostly eat seeds, nuts, fruits, and nectar.
      Some species also eat insects and small animals.

  3. Lifespan:
      4. Larger parrots can live over 50 years
      The Kakapo is the longest-living parrot, with some reaching over 90 years.

  4. Flight and Movement:
      5. Most parrots are strong fliers.
      Kakapos, however, are flightless.
